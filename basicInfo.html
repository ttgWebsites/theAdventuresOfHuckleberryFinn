Basic Info

  • Title
  • The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn
  • Author
  • Mark Twain
  • Published
  • United States. Charles L. Webster & Co. 1884.
  • Genre
  • Bildungsroman, satire, adventure.
  • Setting
  • The novel was set a few decades before the Civil War in the St. Petersburg, Missouri as well in other places along the Mississippi River in Missouri and Arkansas.
  • Point Of View
  • The novel is told exclusively from Huck's point of view.
  • Conflict
  • The main conflict in the novel is between Huck and the constriction he feels from society. He wants to be free, yet he keeps being pressured to become civilized, so he sets out on his own to be free. Later on he runs into another conflict, whether he should turn in his friend Jim, or go against society because he knows Jim is a good man.
